GIS-based optimal siting of Solar PV Plants in Indonesia

A decarbonised electrical supply vision to 2040

Indonesia has set forward ambitious targets in terms of its renewable energy futures. President Prabowo Subianto famously declared plans for Indonesia to achieve net zero emissions by 2050, 10 years ahead of schedule. Traditionally steeped in coal use, this energy transition aims to phase out fossil fuels and expand renewables capacity. Studies suggest that upto 146 billion USD investments may be required for Indonesia to meet its 2030 targets. The objective of this study is to perform a holistic analysis from demand projection, to site suitability analysis for solar PV plant installation and ultimately financial assessment of the projects viability. The end goal is to develop a strategy for solar development that the Indonesian government can use as a road map for its energy transition.

Methodology Overview

The analysis is carried out in the following steps:

  1. A country snapshot is obtained to establish the capacity of existing, in construction, and planned renewable projects in Indonesia.
  2. From the literature, a projection is made of Indonesiaâ€™s national electricity demand in 2040.
  3. Determination of new solar capacity required: From the projected electricity demand, the capacity of new solar plants required is determined based on assumptions of capacity factors of different energy sources and national energy generation targets of the Indonesian government.
  4. A site suitability analysis is carried out including:
    • Identification of criteria: Identification of the various criteria that influence the suitability of a site for solar PV plant installation. These include environmental, social, technical, and economic criteria.
    • Data collection: Gathering of spatial data for each criterion from various sources such as satellite imagery, government databases, and field surveys.
    • Visualizing spatial distribution: Visualizing spatial distribution of the individual criteria.
    • Multicriteria decision making: Multicriteria decision making utilizing analytic hierarchical process (AHP)
    • Identification of solar sites: Identification the number of solar sites required to meet the installed capacity required, their optimal locations and length of transmission lines required for grid connections
  5. Finally, a financial analysis is carried out to establish the economic viability.

The factors determining optimal sites can be categorized as climatic such as solar irradiance, topographic, e.g. elevation, and proximity factors e.g. distance to existing grid infrastructure. Project Overview

This study analyzes potential solar plant investments in Indonesia in line with its national energy transition. The objective is to perform a holistic analysis from demand projection, to site suitability analysis for solar PV plant installation and ultimately financial assessment of the projects viability. The end goal is to develop a strategy for solar development that the Indonesian government can use as a road map for its energy transition.

Key Findings

Number of Solar PV plants required

85 GW of additional capacity required to meet 2040 targets, translating to 3-4 (12km2-sized) new solar PV plants.

Site locations

It is proposed to construct the three solar PV plants across the island of Java that hosts the capital Jakarta. Specifically, the provinces of Jawa Barat, Jawa Tengah and Jawa Timur.

Transmission line length

The total length from proposed sites to nearest transmission lines is approximately 25 km.

Capital expenditure

The total capital expenditure required for the proposed solar PV plants is estimated at USD 100 billion, with a positive Net present value signalling project viability and levelized cost of electricity (LCOE) of USD 0.03/kWh.
